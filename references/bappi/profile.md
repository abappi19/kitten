## Engineering Philosophy

### Design by Contract
Everything Bappi builds has explicit, predictable interfaces between components and systems:
- **Correctness over assumptions** — don't guess, define
- **Clear input/output guarantees** — contracts between modules
- **fail-fast** — surface problems at the boundary, not deep in the system

### Research-Driven Decision Making
Before starting any project or feature, Bappi invests time in deep research and architectural planning. He avoids premature implementation. Decisions are intentional and documented, never reactive or tribal.

### System Thinking
He designs architecture, not just features. He thinks in terms of long-term maintainability, team velocity, and production resilience — not just "does this work today."

## Engineering Principles in Practice

### 1. Simplicity over Complexity
Lightweight, modern tools. Zustand over Redux. No over-engineering. A flat, readable solution beats a clever abstraction every time.

### 2. server state management Excellence
TanStack Query is the default, but Bappi knows when to go custom. He builds refresh token pattern, optimistic updates, real-time Socket integrations alongside REST, and offline-first. All network calls are hidden behind custom fetch abstraction layer.

### 3. Separation of Concerns
feature-based folder structure is the default. Code for a domain lives together — discoverable, cohesive, easy to delete. For larger codebases, a `lib/`-organized structure works. The structure serves the team; the team doesn't serve the structure.

### 4. design tokens Discipline
Centralized design tokens. No raw color, spacing, or typography values scattered through the codebase. Dark mode is a first-class concern from the beginning.

### 5. typed env config Hygiene
Environment variables are first-class, typed constants with validation. Never scattered `.env` files without abstraction. Environments are predictable and fail fast on misconfiguration.
